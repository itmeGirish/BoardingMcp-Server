SUIT FOR PERMANENT INJUNCTION, MANDATORY INJUNCTION (DEMOLITION OF ENCROACHMENT) AND INTERIM INJUNCTION UNDER THE SPECIFIC RELIEF ACT, 1963

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

JURISDICTION

1. The Plaintiff is a person capable of suing and is in possession of the suit land, which forms the subject-matter of this suit. The Defendant is likewise a person capable of being sued.

2. This Hon'ble Court has territorial jurisdiction to entertain and try the present suit inasmuch as the suit land is situate at {{PROPERTY_LOCATION}}, within the local limits of the jurisdiction of this Court, and the encroachment and construction by the Defendant on the Plaintiffs land occurred within such local limits.

3. This Hon'ble Court has pecuniary jurisdiction to try the present suit, the relief sought being injunctive in nature and no monetary claim being pleaded; the suit is maintainable in this Court under the relevant provisions governing suits for injunctions.

FACTS AND EVIDENCE

4. The Plaintiff is the absolute owner of the land described in the title deed, a true copy whereof is annexed herewith and marked as Annexure A, and has been in possession thereof since 2010, as evidenced by the documents and recitals in Annexure A.

5. The Plaintiffs land is plainly identifiable by its boundaries and area, and the Plaintiff has uninterrupted possession since 2010, as evidenced by the possession records and the title deed (Annexure A).

6. The Defendant is an adjacent landowner and, without any lawful entitlement, encroached upon approximately 200 square feet of the Plaintiffs land and has started construction thereupon (the encroached portion is shown in Annexure B).

7. The encroachment and construction by the Defendant threaten the Plaintiffs exclusive possession and enjoyment of the land, and constitute an ongoing continuing wrong requiring urgent intervention of this Court.

8. The Plaintiff has at all material times remained in settled possession of the suit land and has neither consented to nor acquiesced in the encroachment. The Defendant has no legal or equitable right to encroach upon or remain on the Plaintiffs land; no licence or transfer in favour of the Defendant in respect of the encroached area exists.

9. A true copy of the title documents (Annexure A) and a plan/sketch demarcating the encroached portion (Annexure B) are filed with this plaint. A copy of the legal notice issued to the Defendant calling upon them to remove the encroachment, if any, and to cease construction, is Annexure C. If the Defendant replied to the notice, a copy of the reply is Annexure D. The Plaintiff is prepared to place such further documentary or oral evidence as may be required to establish these facts.

10. The encroachment is not only a wrongful act but also a continuing harm, giving rise to a continuing cause of action. The Plaintiff is therefore entitled to interim, permanent, and mandatory relief to preserve the Plaintiffs land and to restore the status quo ante.

11. The Plaintiff seeks a demolition of the encroached construction and a permanent injunction restraining the Defendant, their agents, officers, contractors, successors, assignees, and all others acting on their behalf from encroaching upon or constructing upon the Plaintiffs land in future. The Plaintiff also seeks interim relief to restrain the Defendant from continuing construction or taking any steps towards completing the encroachment until the final disposal of this suit.

12. In the facts and circumstances stated above, the Plaintiff prays for reliefs as hereinafter stated and for such other and further relief as this Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.

LEGAL BASIS

13. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable to be restrained and directed to demolish the encroached portion and to refrain from any future encroachment under Section 36 and Section 37 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963 (Act 47 of 1963). These provisions empower this Court to grant permanent injunctions when the Plaintiff proves title, possession, and a wrongful encroachment by the Defendant, thereby restraining the Defendant from continuing to invade the Plaintiffs rights.

14. In addition, the Plaintiff seeks a mandatory injunction under Section 38(3) of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, for demolition of the encroached portion. Section 38(3) provides for relief in cases of other actionable wrongs where a permanent injunction is sought; in the present case, the encroachment and ongoing construction on the Plaintiffs land constitute such a wrongful act, and the Plaintiff is entitled to a direction for removal/demolition of the encroached construction.

15. Article 113 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963 governs the period of limitation for suits where no period is expressly provided elsewhere. The present suit is filed within the period of three years from the accrual of the right to sue, which accrual arises on the date of encroachment and continues so long as encroachment and construction persist, thereby keeping the cause of action continuing.

CAUSE OF ACTION

16. The cause of action first arose on the date the Defendant encroached upon approximately 200 square feet of the Plaintiffs land and commenced construction thereon, which encroachment remains ongoing. The cause of action is therefore continuing, arising from the date of encroachment and continuing day by day as the encroachment endures and construction proceeds.

17. The cause of action further arose on the date of service of the legal notice (Annexure C), if any, and continues to accrue because the encroachment remains unremoved and the construction persists, thereby sustaining the Plaintiffs right to sue for permanent and mandatory injunctions.

LIMITATION

18. The present suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 113 of the Limitation Act, 1963. The accrual date is the date of encroachment and continuing, such that the suit, filed within three years from the accrual date, is within limitation. The encroachment date is reflected by the facts and annexures referenced above.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

19. This suit seeks permanent and mandatory injunctions and interim relief only; there is no monetary claim. The value for the purposes of jurisdiction and court fee is based on the value of the encroached land, and the court fee is computed at {{COURT_FEE_AMOUNT}} under the applicable Court Fee Schedule for injunctive relief. A statement of court fee paid is annexed to the pleadings.

INTERIM INJUNCTION

20. Until the final disposal of this suit, the Plaintiff prays for an order of interim injunction restraining the Defendant, their agents, servants, contractors, successors, assigns and all others acting on their behalf from continuing the encroachment, from constructing further on the encroached portion, and from taking any steps that would aid or facilitate such encroachment.

PRAYER

21. In the premises, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

    (a) Grant a permanent injunction restraining the Defendant, their heirs, successors, assigns, agents, servants and all those acting on their behalf from encroaching upon or constructing upon the Plaintiffs land, and from in any manner interfering with the Plaintiffs possession and enjoyment of the suit land as described in Annexure A;

    (b) Issue a mandatory injunction directing the Defendant to demolish and remove the encroached construction and restore the status quo ante on the encroached portion within such time as this Court may fix, and to restrain the Defendant from taking any steps to reconstruct or re-encroach on the Plaintiffs land;

    (c) Pass an interim order, pendente lite, restraining the Defendant from continuing the encroachment or commencing construction on the encroached portion until final disposal of this suit, and directing the Defendant to maintain status quo as on the date of this plaint;

    (d) Award costs of the suit to the Plaintiff; and

    (e) Grant such other and further relief as this Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.
