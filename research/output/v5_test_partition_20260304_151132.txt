LEGAL BASIS

18. The Plaintiff submits that as a coparcener and co-owner in the ancestral property, he is entitled to seek partition and separate possession of his lawful share under Order XX Rule 18 CPC read with Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (Sections 6, 8, 10) and Partition Act, 1893 (Sections 2-4). In the alternative, without prejudice to the primary relief, the Plaintiff relies on the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (Sections 6, 8, 10) for coparcenary rights and on the Partition Act, 1893 (Sections 2-4) for partition among co-owners. The property is ancestral and coparcenary in nature and the Plaintiff's 1/3 share entitles him to partition and separate possession.

CAUSE OF ACTION

19. The cause of action arises from the joint ownership and coparcenary rights in the ancestral property and the Defendant's refusal to partition amicably or to recognize the Plaintiff's 1/3 share. 20. The cause of action is rooted in the right of co-owners to partition under the applicable statutes and in the absence of amicable settlement, which necessitates judicial intervention for a preliminary decree under Order XX Rule 18 CPC.
