SUIT FOR PARTITION AND SEPARATE POSSESSION OF ANCESTRAL JOINT FAMILY PROPERTY AND MESNE PROFITS

UNDER ORDER XX, RULE 18 OF THE CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE, 1908
READ WITH SECTIONS 6, 8, 10 OF THE HINDU SUCCESSION ACT, 1956; SECTIONS 2-4 OF THE PARTITION ACT, 1893

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

JURISDICTION

1. The Plaintiff is a Hindu coparcener and co-owner of the joint family property; The Defendants are also co-owners of the same ancestral property.

2. This Hon'ble Court has territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction to try and entertain the present suit since the properties situate at No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore and Survey No. 85, Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural fall within the jurisdiction of this Court, and the cause of action accrued within the local limits of this Court.

FACTS OF THE CASE

3. The Plaintiff and Defendants inherited the aforesaid properties from their deceased father, Shri {{FATHER_NAME}}, who died on {{FATHER_DEATH_DATE}}; The properties are ancestral in nature and remain coparcenary joint family property of the Plaintiff and Defendants.

4. The properties comprise (i) a residential house at No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore measuring 2400 sq.ft.; and (ii) an agricultural land at Survey No. 85, Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural measuring 2 acres. The Plaintiff claims a 1/3rd share in the aforesaid properties and seeks partition and separate possession of his share.

5. The Defendants deny the Plaintiff’s rightful 1/3rd share and refuse to partition the joint family property, thereby depriving the Plaintiff of his legal entitlement.

6. The Plaintiff has been excluded from possession of the aforesaid properties by the Defendants since {{EXCLUSION_DATE}} and has been deprived of his rightful share and enjoyment.

7. The Plaintiff is ready and willing to partition the joint family property by metes and bounds and to give effect to his 1/3rd share, and to obtain separate possession.

8. It is necessary and appropriate that a Commissioner be appointed to effect partition of the aforesaid properties and to file a report before this Hon'ble Court.

CAUSE OF ACTION

9. The cause of action for partition first accrued on the death of Shri {{FATHER_NAME}} and the subsequent denial of partition by the Defendants; The cause of action is continuing as the Defendants persist in denying partition and exclusion continues.

10. The Plaintiff asserts a claim for mesne profits for exclusion from possession since {{EXCLUSION_DATE}} until the date of decree, the amount to be determined by the Court and in accordance with law.

LEGAL BASIS

11. The suit is maintainable under Order XX, Rule 18 CPC for partition and separate possession of the joint ancestral property.

12. In the alternative, the Plaintiff relies on the following statutory provisions:

    (i) Hindu Succession Act, 1956: Section 6, Section 8, Section 10;  
    (ii) Partition Act, 1893: Section 2, Section 3, Section 4;  
    (iii) Indian Succession Act, 1925: Section 211, Section 218.

LIMITATION

13. There is no applicable limitation prescribed for partition; this is a continuing cause of action. If ouster/exclusion is pleaded, the accrual begins from the date of exclusion. No Limitation Act article is cited in this plaint.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

14. The suit is valued for purposes of jurisdiction and court fee at Rs. {{SUIT_VALUE}} (Rupees {{SUIT_VALUE_WORDS}} Only). The requisite court fee of Rs. {{COURT_FEE}} has been paid.

INTEREST

15. The Plaintiff claims mesne profits for exclusion from possession, which may be determined by the Court and the Commissioner, and shall be payable from the date of exclusion until realisation, as per applicable law.

PRAYER

16. In the premises stated hereinabove, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

    (a) Declare that the Plaintiff is entitled to 1/3rd share in the joint ancestral properties described above and to partition the same by metes and bounds;

    (b) Pass a decree for partition and separate possession in terms of the partition so as to grant the Plaintiff his 1/3rd share;

    (c) Appoint a Commissioner under Section 2 of the Partition Act, 1893, and under Order XX, Rule 18 CPC, to effect the partition and submit the report to this Court with a plan of partition and separate possession;

    (d) Pass such preliminary/final decree as may be necessary to give effect to the partition and separate possession;

    (e) Award mesne profits for exclusion from possession from the date of exclusion {{EXCLUSION_DATE}} to the date of decree, and for such future period as the Court may deem fit;

    (f) Award costs of the suit including court fees, advocate’s fees, and any other pre-litigation costs incurred; and
