JURISDICTION

1. This Hon'ble Court has jurisdiction to try and entertain the present suit inasmuch as:
   (a) The suit property, being Flat No. 302, 3rd Floor, Sunshine Apartments, Koramangala, Bengaluru, is situate within the territorial limits of this Hon'ble Court;
   (b) The defendant resides within the local limits of this Court's jurisdiction;
   (c) The cause of action has arisen within the territorial jurisdiction of this Court;
   (d) The suit valuation of Rs.85,00,000/- (Rupees Eighty Five Lakh Only) falls within the pecuniary jurisdiction of this Court.

FACTS OF THE CASE

2. The Plaintiff is a resident of Indiranagar, Bengaluru, and is sui juris and competent to file the present suit.

3. The Defendant is the owner of Flat No. 302, 3rd Floor, Sunshine Apartments, Koramangala, Bengaluru, measuring approximately 1200 sq. ft. of super built-up area (hereinafter referred to as "the suit property").

4. On 10.01.2024, the Plaintiff and the Defendant entered into an Agreement to Sell in respect of the suit property for a total sale consideration of Rs.85,00,000/- (Rupees Eighty Five Lakh Only). A true copy of the Agreement to Sell dated 10.01.2024 is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-A.

5. In terms of the said Agreement, the Plaintiff paid an advance/earnest money of Rs.25,00,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Lakh Only) to the Defendant on 10.01.2024 via RTGS bearing UTR No. HDFC20240110987654. A true copy of the RTGS receipt is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-B.

6. As per Clause _____ of the Agreement to Sell, the balance consideration of Rs.60,00,000/- (Rupees Sixty Lakh Only) was to be paid by the Plaintiff on or before 10.07.2024 against simultaneous execution and registration of the Sale Deed by the Defendant.

7. The Plaintiff has at all material times been and continues to be ready and willing to perform her part of the contract, including payment of the balance consideration of Rs.60,00,000/-.

8. On or about 05.06.2024, the Defendant sent a written communication to the Plaintiff unilaterally refusing to execute the Sale Deed and conveying that he had received a higher offer for the suit property. A true copy of the Defendant's letter dated 05.06.2024 is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-D.

9. The Plaintiff, through her advocate, issued a Legal Notice dated 20.06.2024 calling upon the Defendant to honour his commitment and execute the Sale Deed within 15 days. A true copy of the Legal Notice along with the postal receipt is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-C.

10. Despite receipt of the Legal Notice, the Defendant has failed and refused to execute the Sale Deed, thereby committing breach of the Agreement dated 10.01.2024.

LEGAL BASIS

11. The Plaintiff submits that under Section 10 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, specific performance of a contract shall be enforced when there exists no standard for ascertaining the actual damage caused by the non-performance, or when the act agreed to be done is such that compensation in money for its non-performance would not afford adequate relief.

12. The Agreement to Sell dated 10.01.2024 is a valid, subsisting, and enforceable contract. Immovable property, being unique in nature, cannot be compensated by monetary damages alone, and hence, specific performance is the only adequate remedy available to the Plaintiff.

13. Under Section 16(c) of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, the Plaintiff avers that she has performed and has always been ready and willing to perform her part of the contract. The Plaintiff is not in default under the Agreement and has not done any act subsequent to the contract that would disentitle her from obtaining specific performance.

CAUSE OF ACTION

14. The cause of action for the present suit first arose on 10.01.2024 when the Agreement to Sell was executed, and further arose on 05.06.2024 when the Defendant unilaterally repudiated the contract by refusing to execute the Sale Deed. The cause of action is a continuing one as the Plaintiff remains ready and willing to perform and the Defendant continues to refuse performance.

LIMITATION

15. The present suit is well within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 54 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963, which provides a period of three years from the date fixed for performance. The last date fixed for performance was 10.07.2024. The present suit filed herewith is within the prescribed period.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

16. The suit is valued at Rs.85,00,000/- (Rupees Eighty Five Lakh Only), being the total sale consideration of the suit property. The court fee is paid on the said amount as per the provisions of the Karnataka Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1958, at the applicable ad valorem rate.

INTEREST

17. The Plaintiff claims interest at the rate of 6% per annum on the advance amount of Rs.25,00,000/- from the date of payment (10.01.2024) till the date of execution of the Sale Deed, as damages for wrongful withholding by the Defendant.

PRAYER

18. In the premises stated above, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

(a) Pass a decree of specific performance directing the Defendant to execute and register the Sale Deed in respect of Flat No. 302, 3rd Floor, Sunshine Apartments, Koramangala, Bengaluru, measuring 1200 sq. ft., in favour of the Plaintiff, upon the Plaintiff paying the balance consideration of Rs.60,00,000/-;

(b) Direct the Defendant to deliver vacant and peaceful possession of the suit property to the Plaintiff;

(c) In the alternative, if specific performance cannot be granted, pass a decree for refund of Rs.25,00,000/- along with interest at 18% per annum and damages;

(d) Award the costs of the suit to the Plaintiff;

(e) Grant such other and further relief(s) as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.
