FACTS OF THE CASE

4. The Plaintiff is a reputed businessman residing at {{PLAINTIFF_ADDRESS}} and is known for fair dealing in his business.

5. The Defendant published false and defamatory statements on social media alleging that the Plaintiff had committed fraud in business dealings (the Defamatory Posts). A true copy of the Defamatory Posts is annexed hereto and marked as Annexure A.

6. The Defamatory Posts were widely disseminated and circulated on social media, having been shared more than five hundred times, causing severe reputational damage to the Plaintiff. Proof of dissemination and reach is annexed herewith as Annexure B.

7. The Plaintiff states that he had engaged in legitimate business transactions and conducted himself with integrity; the Defamatory Posts contain false allegations of fraud and are untrue, and were made with the intention to injure the Plaintiffs reputation and business interests.

8. The Plaintiff seeks a mandatory injunction to take down the Defamatory Posts from all social media platforms and to restrain the Defendant from publishing any further defamatory material concerning the Plaintiff.

9. The Plaintiff has not sought to recover any existing profits or damages beyond the stated compensatory and exemplary damages, which are sought in the Prayer below.

10. The cause of action arises on the date of first publication of the Defamatory Posts, i.e., on or about {{PUBLICATION_DATE}}. The Plaintiffs claim for damages is based on the harm caused by that single publication to the Plaintiffs reputation and business standing.

LEGAL BASIS

11. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable in defamation under the Law of Torts (Common Law) for publishing false and defamatory statements that injure the Plaintiffs reputation and business prospects. The civil remedy in defamation includes damages for loss of reputation and reputational harm, and where the defendants conduct is egregious or malicious, exemplary damages may be awarded. The Plaintiff seeks damages for the harm caused by the Defamatory Posts and a mandatory injunction to remove the posts.

12. In addition, the Plaintiff seeks a permanent injunctive relief under the appropriate Civil Procedure and Equitable principles to prevent further publication of defamatory material by the Defendant and to compel takedown of the Defamatory Posts from the social media platforms.

CAUSE OF ACTION

13. The cause of action arises on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}} when the Defendant published the Defamatory Posts alleging fraud by the Plaintiff in business dealings, which statements were communicated to the Plaintiffs customers, business associates and the public, causing substantial damage to the Plaintiffs reputation and business prospects. This is a single-event cause of action; the loss to reputation is a consequence thereof, but the breach (publication) occurred on the single date stated above.

LIMITATION

14. This suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 1 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963, inasmuch as the defaming publication occurred on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}} and the suit is filed within one year from that date.
