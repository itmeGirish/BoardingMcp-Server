LEGAL BASIS

31. The Plaintiff's right to recover possession of the suit schedule property is founded upon the following statutory provisions:

   (a) Section 5 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides for recovery of possession of specific immovable property by a person entitled to possession thereof;

   (b) Section 6 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides for a suit for recovery of possession by a person who has been dispossessed without his consent;

   (c) Section 52 of the Indian Easements Act, 1882, which defines a licence as a grant of permission to do or continue to do something upon or in the immovable property of the grantor, which without such permission would be unlawful, and which does not create an interest in the property;

   (d) Section 62 of the Indian Easements Act, 1882, which provides for revocation of a licence;

   (e) Section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which confers jurisdiction on the Civil Court to try all suits of a civil nature;

   (f) Section 16 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which provides for territorial jurisdiction in suits relating to immovable property;

   (g) Section 20 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which provides for jurisdiction based on the residence or place of business of the Defendant;

   (h) Section 2(12) of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which defines mesne profits;

   (i) Order XX Rule 12 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which provides for the procedure for ascertainment of mesne profits;

   (j) Section 52 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (Doctrine of Lis Pendens), which prevents the Defendant from transferring or creating any interest in the suit property during the pendency of the present suit.

32. It is further submitted that a licensee, upon revocation of the licence, has no right whatsoever to continue in occupation of the property and becomes a trespasser. The grantor of the licence is entitled to recover possession from the licensee after revocation of the licence.
