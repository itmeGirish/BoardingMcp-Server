5. FACTS OF THE CASE
5.1 The Plaintiff and the Defendant(s) are Hindu coparceners and co-owners of the ancestral joint family properties described in Annexure A, having inherited the properties from their deceased father, {{FATHER_NAME}} (hereinafter “the father”). The Plaintiff contends that the father held the properties as joint family assets, acquired by ancestral acquisition, and that the Plaintiff’s share in the joint ancestral property is 1/3rd, as per statutory law.
5.2 The Defendant(s) have denied the Plaintiff’s rightful 1/3rd share and refused to partition the properties, thereby depriving the Plaintiff of his lawful share and his right to seek partition and separate possession.
5.3 Since the aforesaid denial and refusal, the Plaintiff has been excluded from possession of the suit properties pursuant to which the Plaintiff seeks appropriate reliefs, including partition and delivery of possession of his 1/3rd share, along with mesne profits for the period of exclusion.
5.4 The Plaintiff is amenable to partition by metes and bounds and has no objection to an orderly, court-supervised partition with accounts as may be directed by this Hon’ble Court; the Plaintiff seeks appointment of a Commissioner for local inquiry and partition, as provided under the Partition Act and the CPC.

6. CAUSE OF ACTION AND CONTINUING NATURE
6.1 This is a suit for partition and separate possession of ancestral joint family properties. The cause of action first accrued on account of the defendants’ denial of the Plaintiff’s 1/3rd share and refusal to partition, which denial constitutes obstruction to the Plaintiff’s rights as a co-owner. The cause of action is a continuing one, as the Plaintiff’s right to partition subsists and continues to accrue from day to day so long as partition and possession are not provided, and the Plaintiff remains excluded from possession. The continuing accrual continues within the jurisdiction of this Court.

7. OUSTR AND MESNE PROFITS
7.1 The Plaintiff seeks mesne profits for the exclusion from possession from the date of exclusion up to the date of decree, and thereafter as may be directed by this Court, as per the law and Court’s directions. The quantum of mesne profits will be quantified and claimed in the final decree, or as the Court deems fit, based on the accounts and evidence.

8. LIMITATION
8.1 There is no applicable limitation prescribed for partition suits between co-owners; however, if ouster or exclusion is pleaded, the date of exclusion may govern accrual of mesne profits. This Plaintiff pleads exclusion starting from the date asserted in the plaint and seeks relief within the period available under law.

9. LEGAL BASIS
9.1 ORDER XX RULE 18 CPC: Partition of immovable property and separate possession of a share in accordance with law may be ordered by metes and bounds by final decree, upon court direction and after due inquiry or as may be appropriate in the circumstances of the case.
9.2 ALTERNATIVE PLEA WITHOUT PREJUDICE: Hindu Succession Act, 1956, Sec. 6, Sec. 8, Sec. 10; Partition Act, 1893, Sec. 2, Sec. 3, Sec. 4; Indian Succession Act, 1925, Sec. 211, Sec. 218, to the extent they recognize coparcenary rights and succession among Class I heirs and coparceners in relation to the ancestral property and partition claims (where applicable to the facts of this case).
9.3 The Plaintiff seeks partition and separate possession by metes and bounds of the joint family property and a declaration of the Plaintiff’s 1/3rd share in accordance with law.
9.4 The Plaintiff also seeks appointment of a Commissioner to make a partition in terms of Section 13 of the Partition Act or equivalent court-ordered partition under Order XX Rule 18 CPC.

10. IN THE ALTERNATIVE
10.1 Without prejudice to the above, the Plaintiff pleads that if the Court is of the view that a partition by metes and bounds cannot be effected by the Court's direct decree for any reason, the Court may pass a preliminary decree for partition and appoint a Commissioner to localize the partition and report to the Court in terms of the Partition Act.

11. CO-OWNERSHIP AND DEFENCE POINTS
11.1 The Plaintiff is a co-owner and seeks partition; there is no prior partition; the Plaintiff seeks a division and separate possession in accordance with law.

12. MESNE PROFITS (DEMAND)
