LEGAL BASIS

16. That the present suit is filed under and in terms of the following statutory provisions:

      (a) Section 38 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides for the grant of perpetual injunction to prevent the breach of an obligation existing in favour of the applicant, whether expressly or by implication. The Plaintiff has a clear legal right to the suit property, and the Defendant is under an obligation not to encroach upon or interfere with the Plaintiff's peaceful possession and enjoyment thereof.

      (b) Section 39 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides that where the Defendant is in breach of an obligation and such breach causes or threatens to cause injury, the Court may grant a mandatory injunction compelling the Defendant to undo the wrong. The Defendant has committed an illegal encroachment and constructed unauthorized structures on the Plaintiff's land, and justice requires that the Defendant be directed to demolish and remove the same and restore vacant possession.

      (c) Section 36 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides that preventive relief is granted at the discretion of the Court by injunction, temporary or perpetual.

      (d) Section 37 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides that temporary injunctions are regulated by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.

      (e) Order XXXIX Rules 1 and 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which empower the Court to grant temporary injunction in cases where the property in dispute is in danger of being wrongfully alienated or otherwise dealt with, or the Defendant threatens to dispossess the Plaintiff, or the Defendant is about to commit a breach of contract or other injury, or where the Defendant is doing an act in violation of the Plaintiff's rights.

      (f) Section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which provides that Courts shall have jurisdiction to try all suits of a civil nature excepting suits of which cognizance is expressly or impliedly barred.

On the other hand, the Defendant will not suffer any prejudice if restrained from continuing the illegal construction on the Plaintiff's land.
