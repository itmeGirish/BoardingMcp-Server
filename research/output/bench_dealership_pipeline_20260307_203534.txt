SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF DAMAGES FOR ILLEGAL TERMINATION OF DEALERSHIP AGREEMENT

The Plaintiff above named submits as follows:

JURISDICTION

1. This Hon'ble Court has the jurisdiction to try and entertain this suit under Section 9 and Section 20 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, as the cause of action has arisen within the territorial limits of {{CITY}}, {{STATE}}, and the Defendant resides/carries on business within these limits.

2. This suit is a commercial dispute within the meaning of Section 2(1)(c) of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, arising out of a dealership agreement involving trade and commerce. The specified value of the suit is Rs. 50,00,000/-, which is above the threshold limit prescribed under the Commercial Courts Act, 2015.

3. The Plaintiff has complied with the mandatory pre-institution mediation requirement under Section 12A of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, unless exempted due to urgent interim relief. {{STATUS_OF_MEDIATION_COMPLIANCE}}.

AGREEMENT DETAILS AND PERFORMANCE HISTORY

1. The Plaintiff entered into a dealership agreement with the Defendant on {{DATE_OF_AGREEMENT}} for the distribution and sale of the Defendant's products in the territory of {{TERRITORY}}.

2. As per the terms of the agreement, the Plaintiff was required to invest capital and develop the market. The Plaintiff invested a capital amount of Rs. 50,00,000/- towards the development of the territory market.

3. The Plaintiff successfully developed the territory market over a period of 5 years through continuous performance and investment.

4. The agreement stipulated that termination of the dealership could only be effected with a notice period of 6 months.

FACTS OF THE CASE

1. The Defendant terminated the dealership agreement arbitrarily by issuing a termination notice dated {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}}.

2. The termination notice provided only 15 days notice to the Plaintiff, which is in violation of the agreed term requiring 6 months notice.

3. The termination was illegal, arbitrary, and in breach of the contractual obligations owed by the Defendant to the Plaintiff.

4. As a result of the illegal termination, the Plaintiff has suffered significant financial losses including loss of profit, loss of goodwill, and loss on unsold stock.

LEGAL BASIS

1. The Defendant is liable to pay damages under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, for the breach of contract causing loss to the Plaintiff.

2. The Defendant is liable to compensate the Plaintiff under Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, for the breach of the stipulation regarding the notice period for termination.

3. The parties were bound to perform their respective promises under the agreement unless excused under Section 37 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

4. The Defendant has refused to perform their contractual obligation regarding the notice period, constituting a breach under Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

5. The Plaintiff is entitled to interest on the damages claimed under Section 34 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.

CAUSE OF ACTION

1. The cause of action for this suit arose on {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}} when the Defendant illegally terminated the dealership agreement with insufficient notice.

2. The cause of action is a single accrual based on the date of wrongful termination.

LIMITATION

1. This suit is filed within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 55 of the Limitation Act, 1963.

2. The period of limitation is three years from the date of the breach, i.e., the date of illegal termination.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

1. The suit is valued for the purpose of jurisdiction and court fee at Rs. 50,00,000/- being the total amount of damages claimed.

2. The requisite court fee of {{COURT_FEE_AMOUNT}} is paid on the plaint.

DAMAGES SCHEDULE

1. The Plaintiff claims damages under the following heads based on the breach:

(a) Loss of Profit: Rs. 25,00,000/- computed based on the average profits earned over the 5 years of dealership tenure which were lost due to premature termination.
(b) Loss of Goodwill: Rs. 15,00,000/- computed based on the market reputation and customer base developed over 5 years which was appropriated by the Defendant upon termination.
(c) Loss on Unsold Stock: Rs. 10,00,000/- computed based on the value of inventory held by the Plaintiff which cannot be sold due to termination.
