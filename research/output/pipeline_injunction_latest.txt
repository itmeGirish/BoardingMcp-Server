SUIT FOR PERMANENT AND MANDATORY INJUNCTION

1. The Plaintiff is the absolute owner and person in possession of the property more particularly described in the Schedule of Property annexed hereto. The Plaintiff derives title from {{SOURCE_OF_TITLE}} and has been in peaceful possession and enjoyment of the suit property since 2010.

2. The Defendant is the owner of the adjacent property bearing {{DEFENDANT_PROPERTY_DETAILS}}. The Defendant is a neighbor to the Plaintiff and is well aware of the boundaries and extent of the Plaintiff's property.

3. The suit property is fully identified by its boundaries and survey numbers as detailed in the Schedule of Property below. The Plaintiff holds clear title deeds evidencing ownership and has been paying land revenues and taxes regularly.

4. The Plaintiff asserts their right over the suit property based on the Title Deed executed in their favor and continuous possession since 2010.

5. The Defendant has no right, title, or interest whatsoever in the suit property of the Plaintiff.

6. Recently, the Defendant encroached upon 200 sq.ft. of the Plaintiff's land and started construction on the encroached portion. This act of encroachment and construction was done illegally without the consent or permission of the Plaintiff.

7. The cause of action arose on {{DATE_OF_ENCROACHMENT}} when the Defendant encroached upon the suit property and started construction, thereby interfering with the Plaintiff's lawful possession and ownership rights.

8. This Court has jurisdiction to try and entertain this suit under Section 9, Section 16, and Section 20 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, as the suit property is situated within the territorial limits of this Court and the Defendant resides within the jurisdiction.

PLAINTIFF RIGHT AND DEFENDANT INTERFERENCE

9. The Plaintiff has a vested legal right to possess and enjoy the suit property as the absolute owner. The Defendant's act of encroaching 200 sq.ft. and initiating construction constitutes a direct interference with the Plaintiff's lawful possession and title. The Defendant's actions threaten to dispossess the Plaintiff from a portion of the property and alter the nature of the land illegally.

IRREPARABLE HARM AND BALANCE OF CONVENIENCE

10. If the Defendant is not restrained by a decree of permanent and mandatory injunction, the Plaintiff will suffer irreparable loss and injury which cannot be compensated in terms of money. The construction by the Defendant will lead to permanent loss of land and legal complications. The balance of convenience lies in favor of the Plaintiff as the Plaintiff is the recorded owner in possession, whereas the Defendant is a trespasser on the specific encroached portion.

11. The suit is filed within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 113 of the Limitation Act, 1963, as the cause of action arose recently on {{DATE_OF_ENCROACHMENT}} and the suit is filed within three years from said date.

12. The suit is valued at Rs. {{SUIT_VALUATION}} for the purpose of court fee and jurisdiction.

(a) Granting a decree of mandatory injunction directing the Defendant to demolish the construction raised on the encroached 200 sq.ft. of the suit property;
(b) Granting a decree of permanent injunction restraining the Defendant, their agents, servants, or anyone acting on their behalf from encroaching upon or constructing on the suit property in future;
