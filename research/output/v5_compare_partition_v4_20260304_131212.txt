This Hon'ble Court has territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction to entertain the partition suit filed by the Plaintiff, a Hindu coparcener, in respect of ancestral joint family property situate at Bangalore, comprising residential house No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore (2400 sq.ft.) and agricultural land Survey No. 85, Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural (2 acres). FACTS OF THE CASE

1. The Plaintiff and Defendants are members of a Hindu family and hold the property as co-owners in joint family status, each entitled to an undivided share in the ancestral estate inherited from their father, {{FATHER_NAME}}, who is deceased.

2. The Father owned and possessed the residential house at No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore measuring 2400 sq.ft. and agricultural land at Survey No. 85, Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural measuring 2 acres.

3. The Plaintiff claims an equal 1/3 share in the aforesaid joint family property by succession, as the coparcenary rights are recognized under law in the relevant enacting statutes.

4. The Defendants are denying the Plaintiff's 1/3 share and refusing to partition the joint ancestral property, thereby obstructing rightful Partition and Possession.

5. The Plaintiff has on numerous occasions elicited a willingness for partition, but the Defendants have not taken steps to effect partition or grant possession of the Plaintiff's share.

6. The Plaintiff has been continuously prevented from enjoying or occupying his 1/3 share by the Defendants, who have asserted exclusive possession of the entire property.

7. In order to protect the Plaintiff's rights and to obtain a fair and just partition, a Commissioner may be necessary to divide the property and to delineate each co-owner's share, as provided under the applicable Civil Procedure rules.

8. The Plaintiff has prepared and annexed Annexure A (Genealogy Table) and Annexure B (Schedule of Properties) to illustrate the family tree and the properties subject to partition.

9. Copies of the title documents, revenue records, and other proofs of ownership are annexed as Annexure C to establish the joint ownership status and the boundaries of the two assets.

10. The Plaintiff seeks appointment of a Commissioner to carry out a physical partition and to deliver possession of the Plaintiff's 1/3 share, along with a declaration of rights and possession.

11. The Plaintiff further seeks mesne profits for the period of exclusion from possession as a consequential relief, to be determined by the Court after due inquiry, in accordance with Order XX Rule 18 CPC and related provisions.

12. For reference, Annexure A to Annexure C are annexed herewith and marked accordingly.

13. The Plaintiff asserts that the partition and possession are essential to establish and protect the Plaintiff's legal rights as a co-owner.

14. The Plaintiff asserts that there is no valid partition in effect as of the filing of this Suit and that the Defendants have not furnished any partition plan or accounts.

15. This Suit is being filed on the grounds of a continuing cause of action, as the right to partition exists from the time of the father's death and continues until final partition and possession are effected.

LEGAL BASIS

16. Order XX Rule 18 CPC enables a co-tenant or co-owner to file a suit for partition and separate possession, and to appoint a Commissioner to effect such partition where the property is indivisible in kind.

17. The Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (Sections 6, 8, 10) provides coparcenary rights, devolution, and succession among heirs to ancestral property, which support the Plaintiff's entitlement to a 1/3 share.

18. The Partition Act, 1893 (Sections 2, 3, 4) governs partition of joint family or coparcenary property and empowers courts to adjudicate and partition such property among co-owne {{AMOUNT}}. The Indian Succession Act, 1925 (Sections 211, 218) governs devolution of property and can support equitable partition among heirs where necessary.

19. The provisions cited are to be read together to grant a fair partition and to empower the court to appoint a Commissioner to execute the partition and deliver possession of the Plaintiff's 1/3 share, along with any other appropriate relief including mesne profits for exclusion from possession.

LIMITATION

No general period of limitation governs partition suits between co-owners. This filing is made in reliance on the ongoing nature of the right to partition and possession; if ouster or exclusion is pleaded, accrual may be subject to the relevant facts, but there is no fixed limitation period stated herein.
