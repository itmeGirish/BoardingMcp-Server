LEGAL BASIS

19. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable under Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 provides that when a party to a contract has refused to perform, or disabled himself from performing, his promise in its entirety, the promisee may put an end to the contract. In the present case, the Defendant has wrongfully refused to perform its obligations under the dealership agreement by illegally terminating the same without complying with the contractual stipulations and without any justifiable cause, thereby constituting a repudiatory breach. The Plaintiff is entitled to treat the contract as rescinded by the Defendant's breach and claim damages for the losses suffered.

20. The Plaintiff further submits that the Defendant is liable under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 provides that when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, from the party who has broken the contract, compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby, which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it. In the present case, the Defendant's illegal termination of the dealership agreement has caused the Plaintiff loss of profits for the unexpired term, loss of goodwill, wasted capital investment, and loss on unsold stock, all of which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach of contract.

21. Without prejudice to the above and in the alternative, the Plaintiff submits that if the dealership agreement contained any stipulation for liquidated damages or penalty for breach, the Defendant would be liable under Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 provides that when a contract has been broken, if a sum is named in the contract as the amount to be paid in case of such breach, the party complaining of the breach is entitled, whether or not actual damage or loss is proved to have been caused thereby, to receive from the party who has broken the contract reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount so named. The Plaintiff reserves the right to urge this alternative plea if the need arises.

22. The Plaintiff submits that there was no oral variation of the agreement between the parties. The terms of the dealership agreement, including the termination clause, were in writing and any variation required to be in writing as per the agreement itself. The Defendant cannot rely on any alleged oral understanding or waiver to circumvent the express written terms of the agreement. The Plaintiff was not given any opportunity to cure any alleged breach, nor was any prior notice given as contractually mandated. The termination is therefore illegal, void and unenforceable.

23. The Plaintiff submits that no part performance was rendered by the Defendant under the dealership agreement after the illegal termination, and no set-off or counter-claim exists in favour of the Defendant against the Plaintiff in respect of the subject matter of the present suit. The Defendant has wrongfully deprived the Plaintiff of the benefits of the dealership agreement and has unjustly enriched itself at the expense of the Plaintiff.
