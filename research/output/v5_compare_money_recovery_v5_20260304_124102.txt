The Plaintiff seeks relief by way of refund of the advance amount with interest and costs.

LEGAL BASIS

13. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable under Section 65 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, inasmuch as the Plaintiff paid Rs. 20,00,000/- as advance for a business transaction and the entire consideration has failed due to the Defendant's default. Section 65 provides that where money is paid for a consideration which afterwards fails, the person who received the money is bound to restore it. In the present case, the Defendant received the amount and failed to render any consideration, thereby entitling the Plaintiff to recover the amount along with costs and interest.

14. In the alternative, without prejudice to the above, the Plaintiff submits that the Defendant would be liable under Sections 70 and 72 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 for total failure of consideration and related consequences, but the Plaintiff limits his relief to Section 65 as the primary basis for recovery.

16. The cause of action is a continuing one since the Defendant continues to retain the entire advance amount without any lawful justification and has not refunded the same. The Plaintiff is therefore entitled to sue for recovery of the amount and costs, with interest, on a continuing basis.
