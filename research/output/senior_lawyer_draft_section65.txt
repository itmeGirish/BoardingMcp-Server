5. LEGAL BASIS AND GROUNDS

8. The Plaintiff submits that by virtue of Section 65 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when an agreement is discovered to be void, or when a contract becomes void, any person who has received any advantage under such agreement or contract is bound to restore it, or to make compensation for it, to the person from whom he received it. The business transaction between the parties has become void on account of the Defendant's total failure to perform, and the Defendant is therefore bound to restore to the Plaintiff the entire sum of Rs. 20,00,000/- received by him.

9. In the alternative, and without prejudice to the above, the Plaintiff submits that under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for such breach, the amount of loss or damage caused to him, naturally arising in the usual course of things from such breach. The Defendant's default constitutes a breach entitling the Plaintiff to compensation equivalent to the amount advanced.

10. The failure of the Defendant to repay the said amount despite demand constitutes a total failure of consideration, entitling the Plaintiff to claim restitution of the entire sum advanced.
