SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF DAMAGES FOR ILLEGAL TERMINATION OF DEALERSHIP AGREEMENT AND OTHER RELIEFS

1. The Plaintiff is a reputable business entity engaged in the trade and dealership of {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} and is duly registered under applicable laws. The Defendant is a manufacturer/supplier of {{PRODUCT_CATEGORY}} and entered into a commercial dealership agreement with the Plaintiff.

2. Pursuant to the dealership agreement, the Plaintiff invested a capital amount of Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakhs Only) towards setting up the dealership infrastructure and developing the territory market.

3. The Plaintiff diligently developed the market over a period of 5 years, establishing significant goodwill and customer base for the Defendant's products in the assigned territory.

4. The dealership agreement stipulated a mandatory notice period of 6 months for termination of the agreement by either party.

5. On {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}}, the Defendant arbitrarily terminated the dealership agreement by issuing a notice providing only 15 days notice, which is contrary to the agreed contractual term of 6 months notice.

6. The said termination was illegal, arbitrary, and in breach of the terms of the dealership agreement, causing severe financial loss and damage to the Plaintiff's business reputation.

7. As a direct consequence of the illegal termination, the Plaintiff has suffered loss of anticipated profits quantified at Rs. 25,00,000/- (Rupees Twenty-Five Lakhs Only).

8. The Plaintiff has suffered loss of goodwill established over 5 years, quantified at Rs. 15,00,000/- (Rupees Fifteen Lakhs Only).

9. The Plaintiff holds unsold stock of the Defendant's products worth Rs. 10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakhs Only) which has become untenable due to the sudden termination, constituting stock in trade losses.

10. The total damages claimable against the Defendant amount to Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakhs Only) comprising loss of profit, goodwill, and unsold stock losses.

11. The cause of action arose on {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}} when the Defendant illegally terminated the dealership agreement with insufficient notice, breaching the contractual obligations.

12. The cause of action is a single event breach occurring on the date of termination and does not constitute a continuing cause of action.

13. The suit is filed within three years from the date of cause of action as per Article 55 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963.
