SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF POSSESSION OF IMMOVABLE PROPERTY AND MESNE PROFITS

The Plaintiff submits as follows:

JURISDICTION

1. This Court has the jurisdiction to try and entertain this suit as the immovable property described in the Schedule of Property hereunder is situated within the local limits of the jurisdiction of this Court. The cause of action has arisen within the jurisdiction of this Court where the Defendant is in unauthorized occupation of the Plaintiff's property.

FACTS OF THE CASE

2. The Plaintiff is the absolute owner and landlord of the immovable property more particularly described in the Schedule of Property appended to this plaint. The Plaintiff holds clear title to the property evidenced by the Title Deed.

3. The Defendant was inducted into possession of the suit property by the Plaintiff as a tenant under a lease agreement / permissive possession. The Defendant's entry into the property was lawful at the inception based on the agreement between the parties.

4. The period of the permissive possession / lease granted to the Defendant has since expired. Upon the expiry of the said period, the Defendant's right to remain in possession ceased automatically.

5. The tenancy / permissive possession stands determined under Section 111 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882. The Defendant has become an erstwhile tenant whose tenancy has been lawfully determined.

6. After the expiry of the lease period, the Plaintiff issued a legal notice to the Defendant calling upon them to vacate and hand over peaceful possession of the suit property. The Defendant received the said legal notice.

7. Despite receipt of the legal notice and the determination of the tenancy, the Defendant has refused to vacate the suit property. The Defendant is continuing in unauthorized occupation of the property without any lawful authority.

8. The Plaintiff has clear title and the Defendant's occupation is unauthorized. The Defendant has ignored the legal notice served upon them.

LEGAL BASIS

9. This suit is filed under Section 26 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which mandates that every suit shall be instituted by the presentation of a plaint. The particulars of this plaint comply with Order VII Rule 1 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.

10. The Plaintiff seeks recovery of possession under Section 5 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963. This provision entitles a person entitled to the possession of specific immovable property to recover it in the manner provided by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908. The Plaintiff is entitled to possession as the owner and landlord after determination of the tenancy.

11. The lease / permissive possession is governed by the Transfer of Property Act, 1882. Under Section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, the lease is deemed to be month-to-month unless otherwise agreed, and under Section 111 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, a lease of immovable property determines by efflux of the time limited thereby. The facts trigger these provisions as the lease period has expired.

12. The claim for mesne profits is founded on Section 2(12) of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which defines mesne profits as those profits which the person in wrongful possession actually received or might with ordinary diligence have received, together with interest thereon. The Defendant is in wrongful possession day to day.

13. The decree for mesne profits is sought under Order XX Rule 12 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which allows the Court to pass a decree for possession along with an inquiry for mesne profits.

CAUSE OF ACTION

14. The cause of action for this suit first arose on {{DATE_OF_EXPIRY}} when the period of permissive possession / lease expired and the Defendant failed to vacate the property.

15. The cause of action is continuing in nature. The Defendant continues to be in wrongful possession day to day, causing ongoing loss to the Plaintiff. The cause of action continues to arise anew each day the Defendant remains in unauthorized occupation.

LIMITATION

16. This suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 67 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963. This Article provides a period of twelve years for a suit by a landlord to recover possession from a tenant. The limitation period accrues when the tenancy is determined / lease expires. The suit is filed well within the prescribed period.
