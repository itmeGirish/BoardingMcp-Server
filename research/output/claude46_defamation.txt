LEGAL BASIS

16. That the present suit is maintainable under the Law of Torts as recognized and applicable in India. Defamation, being a civil wrong, entitles the injured party to recover compensatory and exemplary damages. The publication of false statements imputing dishonesty, fraud, and criminal conduct to a person constitutes defamation per se, and damage is presumed in law without the necessity of proving special damage, though the Plaintiff herein has also suffered and proved specific pecuniary losses.

17. That the Defendant's conduct constitutes actionable libel under the general principles of the Law of Torts, as the defamatory matter was published in a permanent form (written posts on social media) and was communicated to third parties, causing injury to the Plaintiff's reputation. The ingredients of the tort of defamation are fully satisfied:

    (a) The statements were defamatory in nature, imputing financial fraud and cheating;
    (b) The statements referred to and were understood to refer to the Plaintiff;
    (c) The statements were published to third parties (over 5,000 viewers);
    (d) The statements are false in fact;
    (e) The Plaintiff has suffered actual damage as a consequence thereof.

18. That this suit is instituted under the provisions of Section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC), which confers jurisdiction on the Civil Court to try all suits of a civil nature excepting suits of which cognizance is expressly or impliedly barred. The present suit being one for tortious liability and damages for defamation is a suit of a civil nature triable by this Hon'ble Court.

19. That the Plaintiff claims permanent injunction under the provisions of Sections 36, 37, and 38 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, to restrain the Defendant from publishing, repeating, or further disseminating the defamatory statements or any statements of a similar nature against the Plaintiff. Section 38 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963 empowers the Court to grant a perpetual injunction to prevent the breach of an obligation existing in favour of the applicant, whether expressly or by implication.

20. That the Information Technology Act, 2000, and in particular Section 79 thereof, deals with the liability of intermediaries. While social media platforms may claim exemption under Section 79 as intermediaries, the Defendant, being the originator and publisher of the defamatory content, cannot claim any exemption or immunity under the said provision. The Defendant is the primary tortfeasor who originated and published the defamatory content and is fully liable for the consequences thereof.

LIMITATION

21. That the present suit is well within the period of limitation prescribed under the Limitation Act, 1963. The cause of action first arose on 10.01.2026, when the defamatory statements were published. Under Article 75 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963, the period of limitation for a suit for compensation for defamation is one (1) year from the date when the defamation takes place. The present suit is being filed well within the prescribed period of one year from the date of the defamatory publication. Further, the cause of action is a continuing one, as the defamatory posts remain published online, and each day of continued publication constitutes a fresh cause of action.
