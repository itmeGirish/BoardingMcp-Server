LEGAL BASIS

19. The Plaintiff relies upon the following provisions of law:

   (a) Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which provides that when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby, such compensation for any loss or damage which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it;

   (b) Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which provides that when a contract has been broken, if a sum is named in the contract as the amount to be paid in case of such breach, or if the contract contains any other stipulation by way of penalty, the party complaining of the breach is entitled to receive from the party who has broken the contract, reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount so named or the penalty stipulated for;

   (c) Section 75 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which provides that a person who rightfully rescinds a contract is entitled to compensation for any damage which he has sustained through the non-fulfilment of the contract;

   (d) Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, relating to the effect of refusal of party to perform the whole promise;

   (e) Section 55 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, relating to the effect of failure to perform at fixed time, in contracts in which time is essential;

   (f) Section 2(1)(c)(vii) of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, which defines a commercial dispute to include disputes arising out of agreements relating to franchises;

   (g) Section 12 read with Section 12-A of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, regarding the jurisdiction of Commercial Courts and mandatory pre-institution mediation;

   (h) Order IV Rule 1 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, for institution of the suit.

20. The Plaintiff further relies upon the well-established legal principles enunciated by the Hon'ble Supreme Court that:
   (a) The measure of damages for wrongful termination of a commercial agreement is the loss of profit the aggrieved party would have earned during the unexpired period of the agreement;
   (b) Compensation under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act includes both direct damages and consequential damages which naturally arise in the usual course of business;
   (c) Goodwill developed by a dealer in the products of the principal constitutes a compensable loss upon illegal termination.
