AGREEMENT DETAILS AND PERFORMANCE HISTORY

5. The Plaintiff and Defendant entered into a Dealership Agreement on {{DATE_OF_AGREEMENT}} whereby the Plaintiff was appointed as a dealer for the products manufactured/sold by the Defendant in the territory of {{TERRITORY}}.

6. Pursuant to the said Agreement, the Plaintiff invested a capital amount of Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakhs Only) towards infrastructure, inventory, and market development for the Defendant's products.

7. The Plaintiff diligently performed its obligations under the Agreement for a period of 5 years, developing the territory market and establishing goodwill for the Defendant's brand.

8. The terms of the Dealership Agreement stipulated that termination of the agreement could only be effected upon providing a notice period of 6 months to the Plaintiff.

FACTS OF THE CASE

9. On {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}}, the Defendant terminated the Dealership Agreement abruptly and arbitrarily.

10. The Defendant issued a termination notice providing only 15 days notice, which is in direct violation of the contractual requirement of 6 months notice period.

11. The termination was illegal, void, and in breach of the express terms of the Dealership Agreement, causing significant financial loss to the Plaintiff.

12. As a direct consequence of the illegal termination, the Plaintiff has suffered losses regarding loss of profit, loss of goodwill, and loss on unsold stock.

LEGAL BASIS

13. The Plaintiff relies upon {{CONTRACT_ACT_PROVISION_1}} of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which mandates that parties to a contract must perform or offer to perform their respective promises unless such performance is dispensed with or excused under the provisions of this Act or any other law. The Defendant failed to perform its contractual obligation regarding the notice period.

14. The Plaintiff relies upon {{CONTRACT_ACT_PROVISION_2}} of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which provides that when a party to a contract has refused to perform, or disabled himself from performing, his promise in its entirety, the promisee may put an end to the contract, unless he has signified, by words or conduct, his acquiescence in its continuance. The Defendant's arbitrary termination constitutes a refusal to perform contractual obligations.

15. The Plaintiff relies upon {{CONTRACT_ACT_PROVISION_3}} of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which states that when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, from the party who has broken the contract, compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby. The Plaintiff is entitled to compensation for the losses suffered due to the breach of the notice period clause.

16. The Plaintiff relies upon Section 34 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, which empowers the Court to award interest on the decree amount from the date of the suit to the date of realization.

CAUSE OF ACTION

17. The cause of action for the present suit arose on {{DATE_OF_TERMINATION}}, when the Defendant illegally terminated the Dealership Agreement by issuing a defective notice of only 15 days instead of the agreed 6 months.

18. The cause of action is a single accrual based on the date of breach and continues to subsist as the damages remain unpaid.
