LEGAL BASIS

SECTION: LEGAL BASIS
- Order XX Rule 18 CPC: The Plaintiff seeks a preliminary decree for partition and to have the joint family property divided by metes and bounds, with separate possession of the Plaintiff's share, and to appoint a Commissioner to divide the property. Order XX Rule 18 empowers the Court to pronounce a preliminary decree and to direct partition by metes and bounds where co-owners seek partition of shared property.

- Hindu Succession Act, 1956: Sections 6, 8, 10 (Alternative pleading without prejudice)
  • Section 6: Coparcenary rights accrue to a son (and daughters when applicable) in a joint Hindu family; on the death of the coparcener, the share devolves by succession. The Plaintiff is a coparcener/co-owner and, by virtue of succession in the coparcenary, is entitled to a share in the ancestral property, which requires partition.
  • Section 8: General rules of succession for coparcenary property; the Plaintiff’s 1/3 share is derived from succession as a coparcener in the ancestral property.
  • Section 10: Devolution of interest on the death of a coparcener; the Plaintiff’s right to partition and possession continues notwithstanding status of other coparceners.

- Partition Act, 1893: Sections 2, 3, 4 (Alternative pleading without prejudice)
  • Section 2: Provides for partition of joint property of co-owners; co-owners are entitled to obtain partition and division.
  • Section 3: Enables a partition suit to be brought for the division by metes and bounds.
  • Section 4: Court may decree partition and direct partition by metes and bounds or by sale, as appropriate.

- Indian Succession Act, 1925: Sections 211, 218 (Alternative pleading without prejudice)
  • Section 211 and 218 deal with eligibility of heirs and legal representatives and the administration of estates; they support the Plaintiff's right to share in co-owned ancestral property through legal succession.

SECTION: CAUSE OF ACTION
10. The cause of action arises from the joint ownership and coparcenary rights in the ancestral property and the Defendant's refusal to partition amicably or to recognize the Plaintiff's 1/3 share. The Plaintiff pleads a continuing cause of action until partition is effected and exclusive possession of the Plaintiff's share is granted. The right to partition subsists irrespective of amicable settlement attempts, and judicial intervention is warranted to secure the Plaintiff's rightful share.

11. The cause of action is rooted in the right of co-owners to partition under the applicable statutes (Order XX Rule 18 CPC read with Hindu Succession Act, 1956 and Partition Act, 1893) and in the absence of amicable settlement, which necessitates judicial intervention for a preliminary decree.

LIMITATION

SECTION: LIMITATION
NO LIMITATION APPLIES: Partition between co-owners has no specific limitation; if ouster/exclusion is pleaded, Article 110 (12 years from exclusion) from the Limitation Act may apply; however, in the present pleading, the Plaintiff contends that partition is still due and has not been barred by any applicable limitation. The Plaintiff pleads continuing right to partition and exclusive possession of his 1/3 share.
