LEGAL BASIS

17. The Plaintiff submits that the said Agreement to Sell dated 15.06.2024 is a valid, binding, and enforceable contract within the meaning of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, having been entered into by the free consent of both parties who are competent to contract, for lawful consideration and with a lawful object. The Agreement satisfies all the requirements of a valid contract under Sections 10, 13, 14, and 25 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

18. The Plaintiff is entitled to the relief of specific performance under Section 10 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which provides that specific performance of a contract shall be enforced by the Court where there exists no standard for ascertaining actual damage caused by the non-performance of the act agreed to be done, or where the act agreed to be done is such that compensation in money for its non-performance would not afford adequate relief. Immovable property, being unique in nature, falls squarely within the ambit of Section 10, as no two properties are identical, and monetary compensation cannot be an adequate substitute for the suit property.

19. The Plaintiff further submits that the said Agreement is not one falling within the exceptions enumerated under Section 14 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, and therefore specific performance thereof is enforceable. The contract involves transfer of immovable property, the terms are certain and capable of being specifically performed, and the Plaintiff has not obtained any unfair advantage.

20. The Plaintiff further relies upon Section 16 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, which sets out the personal bars to relief of specific performance. The Plaintiff submits that none of the bars under Section 16 apply to him, as:
    (a) The Plaintiff has not failed to aver and prove that he was ready and willing to perform the essential terms of the contract;
    (b) The Plaintiff has performed or has always been ready and willing to perform the essential terms of the contract on his part;
    (c) The Plaintiff has not become incapable of performing the contract;
    (d) The Plaintiff has not obtained any unfair advantage over the Defendant;
    (e) The Plaintiff has not done any act that is inequitable.

21. The Plaintiff further submits that Section 16(c) of the Specific Relief Act, 1963 (as amended by the Specific Relief (Amendment) Act, 2018) mandates that specific performance shall not be refused merely on the ground that the contract is not enforceable at the instance of the Defendant, or that the Court can award compensation, or that the Plaintiff has obtained any other relief.

22. The Plaintiff relies upon the provisions of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, in particular Sections 54 and 55 thereof, and submits that upon execution of the said Agreement to Sell, the Defendant became bound to sell and transfer the suit property to the Plaintiff and to execute all necessary documents including the Sale Deed.

23. The Plaintiff further submits that the said Agreement to Sell, read with the part-performance provisions under Section 53-A of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, protects the Plaintiff's possession and rights in the suit property, inasmuch as the Plaintiff has performed his part of the contract by paying the earnest money and has always been ready and willing to perform the remaining terms.
