FACTS OF THE CASE

5. The Plaintiff states that his father, Late {{FATHER_NAME}}, was the absolute owner of the suit properties described in Schedule 'A' and Schedule 'B' hereunder, having acquired the same through {{MODE_OF_ACQUISITION}}. The father passed away intestate on {{DATE_OF_DEATH}} at Bangalore, leaving behind the Plaintiff and the two Defendants as his only surviving Class-I legal heirs under Section 8 read with the Schedule to the Hindu Succession Act, 1956.

6. The Plaintiff states that upon the death of the father, the suit properties devolved upon the Plaintiff and the Defendants in equal shares of 1/3rd each by operation of law under Sections 6, 8, and 10 of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956. The Plaintiff is entitled to an undivided 1/3rd share in all the suit properties.

7. The Plaintiff states that the Schedule 'A' property is a residential house bearing No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore, built on a site measuring 2400 sq.ft. The said property is assessed to property tax by the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike and is in the occupation of the Defendants to the exclusion of the Plaintiff.

8. The Plaintiff states that the Schedule 'B' property is agricultural land bearing Survey No. 85, situated at {{VILLAGE_NAME}}, Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural District, measuring 2 acres. The said land is recorded in the Revenue Records in the name of Late {{FATHER_NAME}} and the mutation has not been effected in favour of the legal heirs.

9. The Plaintiff states that after the death of his father, the Plaintiff has been requesting the Defendants to agree to a partition of the suit properties and to give the Plaintiff his lawful 1/3rd share. However, the Defendants have been consistently denying the Plaintiff's rightful share and have been refusing to agree to any partition. The Defendants have been enjoying the entirety of the suit properties to the exclusion of the Plaintiff.

10. The Plaintiff states that the Defendants have been collecting rents from the Schedule 'A' property which is partly let out, and have been enjoying the agricultural income from Schedule 'B' property, without accounting to the Plaintiff for his 1/3rd share of the income.

11. The Plaintiff states that on {{DATE_OF_LEGAL_NOTICE}}, the Plaintiff caused a legal notice to be issued to the Defendants calling upon them to agree to partition and to hand over the Plaintiff's 1/3rd share. A copy of the legal notice is annexed hereto as ANNEXURE-A. The Defendants replied through their advocate denying the Plaintiff's share / did not reply to the notice.

12. The Plaintiff states that the Defendants' refusal to partition is without any lawful justification, and the Plaintiff is left with no alternative but to seek judicial partition through this Hon'ble Court.

CAUSE OF ACTION

14. The cause of action for the present suit arose when the Defendants denied the Plaintiff's rightful 1/3rd share in the suit properties and refused to agree to a partition despite the Plaintiff's repeated requests.

15. The cause of action further arose on {{DATE_OF_LEGAL_NOTICE}} when the Plaintiff issued a legal notice demanding partition, and the Defendants refused to comply.

16. The right to seek partition subsists throughout the period of co-ownership and is not extinguished by joint possession or lapse of time, as no limitation applies to a suit for partition where the co-owners are in joint possession.

LIMITATION

17. The present suit is not barred by limitation. As per the settled law, a suit for partition between co-owners who are in joint possession is not subject to any limitation. No ouster or adverse possession is alleged in this case. The Plaintiff's right to seek partition subsists as long as the co-ownership subsists.
