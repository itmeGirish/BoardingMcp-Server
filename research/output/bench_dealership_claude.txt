LEGAL BASIS

16. The Plaintiff's claim is founded upon Sections 37, 39, 73, and 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. The Defendant's repudiatory breach of the dealership agreement — by terminating without the contractually mandated six months' notice and by failing to buy back unsold stock — entitles the Plaintiff to damages under Section 73, being the loss or damage naturally arising in the usual course of things from such breach.

17. The Plaintiff states that the Defendant has not mitigated the Plaintiff's loss. The Plaintiff, for his part, has taken all reasonable steps to mitigate the loss by attempting to find alternative dealership arrangements, but the nature of the territory-specific dealership and the investment made is such that full mitigation has not been possible.
