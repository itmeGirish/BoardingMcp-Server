PARTIES

2. The Defendant is a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at {{DEFENDANT_ADDRESS}}, and is engaged in the manufacturing and marketing of {{PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION}}.

JURISDICTION

3. This Hon’ble Court has jurisdiction to entertain and try the present suit under Section 2(1)(c) read with Section 6 of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, as the subject matter of the suit is a commercial dispute arising out of a dealership agreement and the value of the subject matter exceeds the specified value.

4. The cause of action arose within the territorial jurisdiction of this Hon’ble Court as the dealership agreement was executed and performed within the jurisdiction of this Court and the impugned termination was communicated to the Plaintiff within this jurisdiction.

FACTS OF THE CASE

5. The Plaintiff and Defendant entered into a Dealership Agreement dated {{AGREEMENT_DATE}}, whereby the Plaintiff was appointed as the exclusive dealer for the Defendant’s products in the territory of {{TERRITORY_NAME}}.

6. As per the terms of the Dealership Agreement, the Plaintiff made an initial capital investment of Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakhs only) towards infrastructure, manpower, marketing, and development of the Defendant’s products in the said territory.

7. The Plaintiff, over a period of five years, diligently developed the market for the Defendant’s products, established a robust distribution network, and significantly enhanced the goodwill and market share of the Defendant in the said territory.

8. Clause {{TERMINATION_CLAUSE_NUMBER}} of the Dealership Agreement specifically provides that either party may terminate the agreement by giving six months’ prior written notice to the other party.

9. On {{TERMINATION_NOTICE_DATE}}, the Defendant arbitrarily and without any justifiable cause, issued a notice of termination to the Plaintiff, granting only 15 days’ notice, in blatant violation of the express terms of the Dealership Agreement.

10. The Plaintiff immediately protested against such illegal and arbitrary termination and requested the Defendant to withdraw the notice and adhere to the contractual notice period of six months. However, the Defendant failed and neglected to do so.

11. As a result of the Defendant’s unlawful and abrupt termination, the Plaintiff has suffered severe losses, including but not limited to loss of anticipated profits, loss of goodwill, and being left with unsold stock.

BREACH OF CONTRACT

12. The Defendant’s action in terminating the Dealership Agreement with only 15 days’ notice, instead of the contractually mandated six months’ notice, amounts to a clear and deliberate breach of contract under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

13. The Plaintiff was ready and willing to perform its obligations under the Dealership Agreement and has not committed any breach thereof.

14. The Defendant’s arbitrary termination has caused the Plaintiff to suffer damages, for which the Defendant is liable to compensate the Plaintiff.

CLAIM FOR DAMAGES

15. By reason of the Defendant’s breach, the Plaintiff has suffered the following losses:

    a) Loss of anticipated profits for the remaining period of the agreement: Rs. 25,00,000/- (Rupees Twenty-Five Lakhs only);

    b) Loss of goodwill and reputation built over five years: Rs. 15,00,000/- (Rupees Fifteen Lakhs only);

    c) Value of unsold stock remaining with the Plaintiff: Rs. 10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakhs only).

16. The total amount claimed by the Plaintiff from the Defendant on account of the above heads is Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakhs only), together with interest thereon at the rate of {{INTEREST_RATE}}% per annum from the date of termination till realization.

CAUSE OF ACTION

17. The cause of action for the present suit arose on {{TERMINATION_NOTICE_DATE}}, when the Defendant issued the illegal termination notice, and continues to subsist as the Defendant has failed to compensate the Plaintiff for the losses suffered.

LIMITATION
