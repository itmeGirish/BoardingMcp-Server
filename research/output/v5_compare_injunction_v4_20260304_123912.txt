PLAINT FOR PERMANENT INJUNCTION AND MANDATORY INJUNCTION

JURISDICTION

1. This Hon'ble Court has territorial jurisdiction since the Defendant resides at {{DEFENDANT_ADDRESS}} which lies within the local limits of this Court and the encroachment occurred on the Plaintiff's land within these jurisdictional limits.

FACTS OF THE CASE

4. The Plaintiff is the owner of the suit land and has been in possession since 2010; the title is evidenced by a title deed in the Plaintiff's name, a copy of which is annexed as Annexure A.

5. The Defendant is the adjacent landowner and is thus in a position to exercise influence over the Plaintiff's boundary area.

6. On or about {{ENCROACHMENT_DATE}}, the Defendant encroached upon approximately 200 square feet of the Plaintiff's land and commenced construction thereon.

7. The encroached portion lies along the common boundary between the two parcels of land and is demarcated in the site plan accompanying this plaint.

8. The Plaintiff has been in exclusive possession of the entire suit land since 2010 and has unfettered title thereto, as evidenced by the title deed (Annexure A).

9. The encroachment is visible on the ground and corresponds to a clearly identifiable portion of Plaintiff's land marked in Annexure B.

10. The encroachment and construction interfere with the Plaintiff's quiet possession and enjoyment of his property and amount to a continuing and actionable wrong.

11. The Plaintiff has not encroached upon or claimed any portion of the Defendant's land, and there is no consent or license from the Plaintiff for any such encroachment.

12. The Plaintiff seeks to protect his rights by restraining the Defendant from continuing the encroachment and by demolishing the encroached construction, so as to restore the status quo ante and prevent any further encroachment.

LEGAL BASIS

13. Section 36 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963 empowers the court to grant a perpetual injunction restraining a person from doing an act injurious to the rights of another in respect of immovable property, where such injury has occurred or is imminent. The Plaintiff seeks such relief to prevent continued encroachment and to safeguard his title and possession.

14. Section 38 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963 provides that where injunction is sought for actionable wrongs, the court may grant such injunction and may direct appropriate relief, including a mandatory injunction to demolish illegal constructions and to restore the prior position where warranted. In the present case, encroachment and construction on the Plaintiff's land constitute an actionable wrong, and the facts justify the grant of both permanent and mandatory injunction.

15. Article 113 of the Limitation Act, 1963 prescribes a three-year period for suits for which no period is provided elsewhere; the right to sue accruing on {{ENCROACHMENT_DATE}} is within this period for instituting an injunction suit.

CAUSE OF ACTION

17. Further accrual: The cause of action further arose on {{ENCROACHMENT_DATE}} and on each subsequent day thereafter as the encroachment and construction continued, causing ongoing injury to the Plaintiff's ownership and possession.

18. Continuing nature: The cause of action is a continuing one, as the Defendant continues to encroach and maintain the structure on Plaintiff's land, which threatens further encroachment and persistent harm to the Plaintiff's rights.
