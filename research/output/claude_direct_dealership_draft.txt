3. LEGAL BASIS

3.1 The Dealership Agreement dated {{AGREEMENT_DATE}} constitutes a valid and binding contract between the parties within the meaning of Sections 2(h), 10 and 11 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. The Agreement was entered into with free consent, for lawful consideration and with a lawful object.

3.2 The Defendant's act of terminating the Agreement without complying with the contractual prerequisites — namely, without serving the mandatory {{NOTICE_PERIOD}} months' notice, without specifying any material breach, and without affording the Plaintiff any opportunity to cure any alleged breach — constitutes a clear and material breach of the Agreement by the Defendant within the meaning of Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

3.3 Under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation, such amount as would naturally arise in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it. The compensation claimed by the Plaintiff represents the direct and proximate loss naturally arising from the Defendant's breach.

3.4 Under Section 55 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when a party to a contract promises to do a certain thing at or before a specified time and fails to do so, the contract becomes voidable at the option of the promisee. The Defendant, by terminating the Agreement before its expiry without lawful cause, has repudiated its obligations and the Plaintiff is entitled to damages.

3.5 Under Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, in the alternative, even if the exact quantum of loss is difficult to ascertain, the Plaintiff is entitled to reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount mentioned in the contract for breach thereof.

3.6 The Plaintiff's claim for loss of profits is supported by the principle of expectation damages, being the profits the Plaintiff would have earned during the unexpired term of the Agreement but for the Defendant's wrongful termination.

3.7 The Plaintiff's claim for loss of goodwill is justified inasmuch as the Plaintiff invested substantial time, effort and resources in developing the brand's market presence in the territory, which goodwill stands destroyed by the arbitrary termination and appointment of a new dealer.

4.2 The cause of action further arose and accrued on {{PLAINTIFF_REPLY_DATE}} when the Defendant failed to respond to the Plaintiff's letter protesting the illegal termination and requesting reinstatement or compensation.

4.3 The cause of action further arose and continued to subsist on {{LEGAL_NOTICE_DATE}} when the Defendant, despite receipt of the legal notice, failed and refused to compensate the Plaintiff for the losses suffered.

4.4 The cause of action is a continuing one as the Defendant continues to wrongfully retain the benefit of the Plaintiff's capital investment and market development efforts, and continues to deprive the Plaintiff of the dealership business and the profits flowing therefrom, and the cause of action continues to accrue and subsist from day to day within the territorial jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court.

5. LIMITATION

5.1 The present suit is well within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 55 of the Limitation Act, 1963, which provides a limitation period of three years for suits for compensation for breach of any contract, express or implied, not expressly provided for in the Schedule.

5.2 The cause of action for the present suit arose on {{TERMINATION_DATE}} when the Defendant breached the Agreement by issuing the illegal termination. The present suit is being filed within three years from the said date and is therefore well within limitation.
