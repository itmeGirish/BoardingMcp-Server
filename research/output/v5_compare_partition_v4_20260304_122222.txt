LEGAL BASIS

13. Order XX Rule 18 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 empowers a Court in a suit for partition and separate possession to determine the shares and, if necessary, to direct partition by metes and bounds and to appoint a Commissioner to effect such partition.

14. Hindu Succession Act, 1956: Sections 6, 8 and 10 govern coparcenary rights and devolution of joint family property; on death of a coparcener, the property devolves by survivorship among the coparceners and female heirs as applicable, preserving the coparcenary rights in the partition context.

15. Partition Act, 1893: Sections 2, 3, 4 provide for partition of estates and the manner of partition and the role of the Court in effectuating such partition.

16. Indian Succession Act, 1925: Sections 211 and 218 relate to the mode of devolution of property on death and the rights of heirs; these provisions underpin hereditary rights in joint family property and support the Plaintiff's entitlement to a share in the partition.

17. The pleadings do not seek any sale of property at this stage; the primary relief is partition by metes and bounds with separate possession of the Plaintiff’s 1/3 share, while reserving the remaining shares for final judicial allotment.
