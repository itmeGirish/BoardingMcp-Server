LEGAL BASIS

18. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable under Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 39 provides that when a party to a contract has refused to perform, or disabled himself from performing, his promise in its entirety, the promisee may put an end to the contract. In the present case, the Defendant has wrongfully repudiated the dealership agreement by issuing the illegal termination notice dated {{TERMINATION_DATE}}, thereby disabling itself from performing its obligations under the agreement. The Plaintiff is entitled to treat the contract as rescinded by the Defendant's breach and claim damages for the losses suffered.

19. The Plaintiff further submits that the Defendant is liable under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 73 provides that when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, from the party who has broken the contract, compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby, which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it. The Plaintiff has suffered loss of profits for the unexpired term, loss of goodwill, wastage of capital investment, and loss on unsold stock, all of which naturally arose from the Defendant's illegal termination and were within the contemplation of the parties at the time of entering into the agreement. The Defendant is therefore liable to compensate the Plaintiff for the said losses.

20. Without prejudice to the above and in the alternative, the Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is also liable under Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Section 74 provides that when a contract has been broken, if a sum is named in the contract as the amount to be paid in case of such breach, or if the contract contains any other stipulation by way of penalty, the party complaining of the breach is entitled, whether or not actual damage or loss is proved to have been caused thereby, to receive from the party who has broken the contract reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount so named or, as the case may be, the penalty stipulated. If the dealership agreement contains any penalty clause or stipulated damages clause, the Plaintiff is entitled to recover the same. Alternatively, the Plaintiff is entitled to recover the actual damages suffered, which have been particularised in the Schedule of Damages.

21. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant's action in terminating the dealership agreement is also opposed to public policy and principles of fair business practice. The Defendant, having induced the Plaintiff to make substantial investments and develop the market, cannot be permitted to arbitrarily terminate the agreement and benefit from the Plaintiff's efforts without compensating the Plaintiff. The Defendant's conduct amounts to unfair trade practice and is liable to be deprecated by this Hon'ble Court.

22. The Plaintiff anticipates that the Defendant may raise defenses regarding alleged breach by the Plaintiff or termination rights. The Plaintiff specifically pleads that: (a) no material breach was committed by the Plaintiff as evidenced by the performance records and awards received; (b) even if any minor breach occurred, the mandatory cure period under the agreement was not provided; (c) no forfeiture clause exists in the agreement that would permit the Defendant to retain the benefits of Plaintiff's investment without compensation; (d) the Defendant has not raised any counter-claim for damages in response to the legal notice.

26. The present suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 55 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963. The cause of action having first arisen on {{TERMINATION_DATE}} when the Defendant breached the dealership agreement by issuing the termination notice, and the suit being filed within three years from the said date, the present suit is well within limitation.
