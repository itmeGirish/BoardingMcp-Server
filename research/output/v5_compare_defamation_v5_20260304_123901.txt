SUIT FOR DAMAGES FOR DEFAMATION AND MANDATORY INJUNCTION

UNDER THE LAWS OF TORTS (DEFAMATION)

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

JURISDICTION

1. The Plaintiff is a person sui juris and competent to institute the present suit. The Defendant is also a person sui juris and can be sued.

2. This Hon'ble Court has territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction to try and entertain the present suit as the defamatory statements were published on social media accessible within the jurisdiction of this Court and the Plaintiff resides within such jurisdiction; the cause of action arose within the local limits of this Court as the publication occurred in {{PUBLICATION_PLACE}} on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}}.

3. This Hon'ble Court has pecuniary jurisdiction to try the present suit, the subject matter and claim for damages amounting to Rs. 15,00,000 (Rupees Fifteen Lakh Only), which is within the pecuniary limits prescribed for this Hon'ble Court.

FACTS OF THE CASE

4. The Plaintiff, {{PLAINTIFF_NAME}}, is a reputed businessman, aged about {{PLAINTIFF_AGE}} years, Occupation: {{PLAINTIFF_OCCUPATION}}, residing at {{PLAINTIFF_ADDRESS}} and engaged in {{BUSINESS_DETAILS}}.

5. On or about {{PUBLICATION_DATE}}, the Defendant published false and defamatory statements on social media alleging that the Plaintiff committed fraud in business dealings. The posts were accessible to the public and were shared/reshared more than 500 times, as evidenced by Annexure B, causing grave reputational damage to the Plaintiff.

6. The statements published by the Defendant were false and unfounded.

7. As a result of the publication, the Plaintiff has suffered grave reputational harm, loss of clientele and business opportunities, and mental distress.

8. The Plaintiff served a legal notice dated {{NOTICE_DATE}} calling upon the Defendant to retract and remove the defamatory posts; a true copy of the notice is annexed hereto and marked as Annexure C. The Defendant has not complied with the demands in the notice.

9. Despite receipt of the said notice, the Defendant has continued to permit, facilitate, and fail to remove the defamatory posts, thereby prolonging and aggravating the Plaintiff's harm.

LEGAL BASIS

10. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable for defamation under the Law of Torts. The civil wrong of defamation arises from the unprivileged publication of false statements concerning the Plaintiff to third parties, causing harm to reputation.

11. The Plaintiff seeks damages for defamation, including compensatory damages for reputational harm and exemplary damages for malice or reckless disregard for the Plaintiff's rights, to be assessed by this Court.

12. In addition, the Plaintiff pleads for a mandatory injunction under the Court's inherent and statutory powers to compel the takedown and permanent removal of all defamatory posts from social media and to restrain further publication of defamatory statements pending final disposal of the suit.

CAUSE OF ACTION

13. The cause of action arose on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}} when the Defendant published the defamatory posts on social media alleging fraud in business dealings.

14. The Plaintiff has served a legal notice as described above and the Defendant has not withdrawn or retracted the statements, nor removed the posts, thereby sustaining and continuing the harm.

LIMITATION

15. The suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 1 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963; the cause of action accrued on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}} and the suit is filed within one year from that date.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

16. The suit is valued at Rs. 15,00,000 (Rupees Fifteen Lakh Only) for purposes of jurisdiction and court fee. The requisite court fee has been paid on this plaint in accordance with the Court Fees Act, 1870.

PRAYER

17. In the premises stated hereinabove, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be graciously pleased to:

    (a) Dismiss the Defendant's stand and hold the Defendant liable for defamation;

    (b) Pass a decree in favour of the Plaintiff for compensatory damages of Rs 10,00,000 (Rupees Ten Lakh Only) for reputational harm and loss of business opportunities;

    (c) Award exemplary damages of Rs 5,00,000 (Rupees Five Lakh Only) for malice and deliberate disregard of the Plaintiff's rights;

    (d) Grant a mandatory injunction directing the Defendant to take down and permanently remove all defamatory posts from social media and to restrain publication of further defamatory statements pending disposal of the suit;

    (e) Grant costs of the suit, including advocate's fees and all pre-litigation costs incurred; and

    (f) Pass such other and further relief as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.
