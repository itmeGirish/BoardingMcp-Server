BREACH OF CONTRACT AND LEGAL BASIS

12. That the Defendant has committed a fundamental and material breach of the Dealership Agreement by terminating the same in flagrant violation of the contractual terms, particularly the mandatory six-month notice period. The said breach attracts the following provisions of law:

    (a) Section 37 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 — The parties to a contract must either perform, or offer to perform, their respective promises. The Defendant has failed to perform its obligation of providing the stipulated six-month notice period before termination;

    (b) Section 39 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 — When a party to a contract refuses to perform, or disables himself from performing, his promise in its entirety, the promisee may put an end to the contract. The Defendant's arbitrary termination with only fifteen days notice amounts to a refusal to perform the contract according to its terms;

    (c) Section 55 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 — When a party to a contract promises to do a certain thing at or before a specified time and fails to do so, the contract becomes voidable at the option of the promisee. The Defendant's failure to provide the contractually mandated notice period renders the termination wrongful;

    (d) Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 — When a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for loss or damage caused to him thereby, such compensation for any loss or damage which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it. The Plaintiff is entitled to compensation for all losses naturally flowing from the Defendant's breach;

    (e) Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 — When a contract has been broken, if a sum is named in the contract as the amount to be paid in case of such breach, or if the contract contains any other stipulation by way of penalty, the party complaining of breach is entitled to receive from the party who has broken the contract reasonable compensation not exceeding the amount so named, whether or not actual damage or loss is proved to have been caused thereby.
