FACTS

4. The Plaintiff, {{PLAINTIFF_NAME}}, is a reputed businessman engaged in substantial commercial activities, with a place of business at {{PLAINTIFF_ADDRESS}}.

5. The Defendant, {{DEFENDANT_NAME}}, published false and defamatory statements on social media alleging that the Plaintiff committed fraud in business dealings. The statements were made publicly on social media platforms accessible to third parties.

6. The statements referred to in paragraph 5 were shared by readers and users more than 500 times, thereby achieving wide dissemination and causing substantial reputational damage to the Plaintiff, who is a reputed businessman.

7. The Plaintiff has always conducted his business with integrity and in accordance with applicable laws and norms. The false statements published by the Defendant have impugned his professional honesty and business reputation, causing loss of goodwill, client trust, and business opportunities.

8. The Plaintiff immediately confronted the Defendant and sought an explanation and withdrawal of the false statements. The Defendant declined to retract or correct the statements and refused to remove them from the social media platforms.

9. The Plaintiff, through his Advocate, served a legal notice dated {{NOTICE_DATE}} upon the Defendant calling upon him to remove the defamatory posts and to refrain from further publication, a true copy of which is annexed hereto and marked as Annexure D. A response, if any, from the Defendant is annexed as Annexure E.

10. The posts continue to be accessible to the public, and the Defendant continues to benefit from the ongoing publication, thereby aggravating the harm to the Plaintiff’s reputation.

11. The Plaintiff seeks immediate redress in the form of damages for defamation, in addition to a mandatory injunction directing the Defendant to take down the defamatory posts from all social media platforms and to prevent further publication.

LEGAL BASIS

12. The Plaintiff submits that the Defendant is liable in tort for defamation, which damages the Plaintiff’s reputation and causes harm to his business interests. In defamation, the Plaintiff is entitled to damages for loss of reputation, along with exemplary damages where the Defendant’s conduct is found to be wilful, malicious, or high-handed in nature, and where such conduct warrants exemplary punishment.

13. The Plaintiff also seeks a mandatory injunction to prevent further breach of the Plaintiff’s rights by directing the Defendant to take down the defamatory posts and to restrain further publication of defamatory material. The Court has the power to grant a mandatory injunction to prevent breach and to compel the defendant to perform acts as may be necessary to protect the plaintiff’s rights, including removal of defamatory material from public fora (Order 39 CPC and relevant provisions governing injunctions).

14. The Plaintiff pleads for compensation for reputational damage, mental distress, and business harm arising from publication of false statements that a) allege fraud in business dealings; b) were disseminated widely (over 500 uses); and c) have caused substantial harm to the Plaintiff’s standing in the business community.
