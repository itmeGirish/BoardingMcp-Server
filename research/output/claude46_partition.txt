FACTS OF THE CASE

5.  The Plaintiff states that Late Shri {{FATHER_NAME}} was the absolute owner of the immovable properties described in Schedule "A" and Schedule "B" hereinbelow. The said properties were ancestral in nature and constituted the joint family property of the Hindu Undivided Family of Late Shri {{FATHER_NAME}}.

6.  The Plaintiff states that Late Shri {{FATHER_NAME}} departed this life on {{DATE_OF_DEATH}} at Bangalore, leaving behind the Plaintiff, the Defendant No. 1, and the Defendant No. 2 as his only surviving legal heirs. The deceased died intestate, that is, without leaving any Will, testament, or codicil.

7.  The Plaintiff states that upon the death of Late Shri {{FATHER_NAME}}, the suit properties devolved by inheritance and succession upon the Plaintiff, the Defendant No. 1, and the Defendant No. 2 in equal shares, each being entitled to an undivided one-third (1/3rd) share therein, by virtue of the provisions of Sections 6, 8, and 10 of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956.

8.  The Plaintiff states that ever since the death of the said Late Shri {{FATHER_NAME}}, all the three legal heirs have been in joint possession and enjoyment of the suit properties. No partition, whether by metes and bounds or otherwise, has ever taken place amongst the parties in respect of the suit properties.

9.  The Plaintiff states that the Schedule "A" property, namely the residential house situated at No. 42, 3rd Cross, Jayanagar, Bangalore, measuring approximately 2400 sq.ft., is presently in the occupation of the Defendant No. 1, who has been exclusively occupying the same and has been collecting the rents and profits therefrom without accounting to the Plaintiff or sharing the same.

10. The Plaintiff states that the Schedule "B" property, namely the agricultural land bearing Survey No. 85, situated at Anekal Taluk, Bangalore Rural District, measuring approximately 2 acres, is presently in the possession and control of the Defendant No. 2, who has been cultivating and enjoying the same to the total exclusion of the Plaintiff.

11. The Plaintiff states that on or about {{DATE_OF_DEMAND}}, the Plaintiff made a demand upon the Defendants requesting an amicable partition of the suit properties and allotment of the Plaintiff's rightful one-third (1/3rd) share therein. However, the Defendants categorically refused to accede to the said request and have been denying the Plaintiff's rightful share in the suit properties.

12. The Plaintiff states that the Defendants have been acting in concert and have refused to recognise the Plaintiff's undivided one-third (1/3rd) share in the suit properties. The Defendants have been treating the suit properties as their own exclusive properties and have been enjoying the rents, profits, and income therefrom without rendering any account to the Plaintiff or giving the Plaintiff any share of the same.

13. The Plaintiff states that despite repeated oral and written requests, including a legal notice dated {{DATE_OF_LEGAL_NOTICE}} sent through registered post with acknowledgment due, the Defendants have failed and refused to partition the suit properties and deliver to the Plaintiff the Plaintiff's rightful one-third (1/3rd) share by metes and bounds.

14. The Plaintiff states that the Defendants have no right, title, or interest in the Plaintiff's undivided one-third (1/3rd) share in the suit properties, and the continued denial of the Plaintiff's share amounts to an ouster and dispossession of the Plaintiff from the Plaintiff's rightful share.

15. The Plaintiff states that the suit properties are capable of being partitioned by metes and bounds and the Plaintiff is entitled to a partition and separate possession of the Plaintiff's one-third (1/3rd) share in the suit properties.

LEGAL BASIS

16. The Plaintiff submits that under Section 6 of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (as amended by the Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005), the Plaintiff, being a coparcener by birth, has a right in the joint family property equal to that of the other coparceners.

17. The Plaintiff submits that under Sections 8 and 10 of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956, upon the death of a male Hindu dying intestate, the property devolves upon the heirs specified in Class I of the Schedule to the said Act. The Plaintiff, the Defendant No. 1, and the Defendant No. 2, being children of the deceased and falling within Class I heirs, are each entitled to an equal share.

18. The Plaintiff submits that under Section 2 of the Partition Act, 1893, any person holding property as a co-owner is entitled to a partition thereof and to obtain separate possession of the share to which the co-owner is entitled.

19. The Plaintiff submits that this suit is maintainable under Section 9 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, and that the partition and allotment of shares may be effected in the manner provided under Order XX Rule 18 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, with the assistance of a Commissioner appointed by this Honourable Court.

LIMITATION

23. The present suit is well within limitation. A suit for partition is governed by Article 65 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963, which prescribes a period of 12 years from the date when the possession of the Defendant becomes adverse to the Plaintiff. In the present case, the Plaintiff has been in constructive joint possession of the suit properties, and the denial of the Plaintiff's share is of recent origin. The suit is, therefore, within time and not barred by limitation.

24. Without prejudice to the above, the Plaintiff submits that the right to seek partition is an incident of co-ownership and is not extinguished by mere lapse of time unless there is a clear ouster coupled with adverse possession for the full statutory period, which has not occurred in the present case.
