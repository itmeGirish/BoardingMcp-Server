5. The father of the Plaintiff and Defendant, Late {{FATHER_NAME}}, died on {{FATHER_DEATH_DATE}} leaving behind coparceners in the form of the Plaintiff and the Defendant, who have continued to hold the subject properties as coparceners in a joint Hindu family. The Plaintiff asserts a 1/3 share in the joint family properties and seeks partition and separate possession of his share. The Defendants, however, deny the Plaintiff's 1/3 share and obstruct partition.

6. The Plaintiff seeks partition and separate possession of his 1/3 share in the joint family properties as described in Annexure A.

7. LEGAL BASIS

11. The Plaintiff relies on:
- ORDER XX RULE 18, CPC: For partition and separate possession of jointly held property where a share is claimed.
- Hindu Succession Act, 1956: Sections 6, 8, and 10, for coparcenary rights and succession to joint family property.
- Partition Act, 1893: Sections 2, 3, and 4, for the operation and process of partition and appointment of a Commissioner.
- Indian Succession Act, 1925: Sections 211 and 218 (as applicable to succession and devolution of properties among coparceners).

12. The Plaintiff submits that, on the facts stated, the Plaintiff is entitled to an immediate partition of the joint family property and separate possession of his 1/3 share, and the Court should appoint a Commissioner to effect the partition in accordance with law.
