LEGAL BASIS

13. That under Section 65 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when an agreement is discovered to be void, or when a contract becomes void, any person who has received any advantage under such agreement or contract is bound to restore it, or to make compensation for it, to the person from whom he received it. The present case involves a total failure of consideration inasmuch as the Defendant received the advance of Rs.20,00,000/- but completely failed to render any performance in return. The Defendant is therefore bound to restore the said sum to the Plaintiff.

14. That under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for the loss or damage caused to him thereby, such compensation for any loss or damage caused to him thereby which naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach. The Plaintiff has suffered loss and damage on account of the Defendant's breach, including loss of use of the sum of Rs.20,00,000/- for the period during which it has been wrongfully retained by the Defendant. The Plaintiff is entitled to compensation by way of interest on the said sum.

15. That under Section 70 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, where a person lawfully does anything for another person, or delivers anything to him, not intending to do so gratuitously, and such other person enjoys the benefit thereof, the latter is bound to make compensation to the former in respect of, or to restore, the thing so done or delivered. The Plaintiff paid Rs.20,00,000/- to the Defendant for a specific purpose which has failed; the Defendant is bound to restore the same.

16. That under Section 72 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, a person to whom money has been paid by mistake or under coercion must repay it. The advance paid by the Plaintiff, the consideration for which has wholly failed, must be returned to the Plaintiff.

17. That the Plaintiff is further entitled to pendente lite and future interest at the rate of 12% per annum (or at such rate as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit) on the principal sum of Rs.20,00,000/- from the date of payment till the date of realisation, on account of the wrongful retention of the Plaintiff's funds by the Defendant.
