SUIT FOR DAMAGES FOR DEFAMATION AND MANDATORY INJUNCTION TO TAKE DOWN DEFAMATORY POSTS
UNDER THE LAW OF TORTS (Defamation) AND THE SPECIFIC RELIEF ACT, 1963

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

JURISDICTION

1. The Plaintiff is a person sui juris and competent to institute the present suit. The Defendant is also a person sui juris and can be sued.

2. This Hon'ble Court has territorial jurisdiction to try and entertain the present suit inasmuch as the defamatory posts were published and circulated within the jurisdiction of this Court and the Plaintiff's cause of action arose within such territorial limits.

3. This Hon'ble Court has pecuniary jurisdiction to try the present suit, the suit being valued at {{AMOUNT}} (Rupees Fifteen Lakh Only), which is within the pecuniary limits prescribed for this Court.

FACTS OF THE CASE

4. The Plaintiff is a reputed businessman residing at {{PLAINTIFF_ADDRESS}} and has built goodwill in the business community over several yea {{AMOUNT}}. The Defendant published false and defamatory statements on social media alleging that the Plaintiff committed fraud in business dealings.

5. The statements were published on social media and circulated widely, being shared more than 500 times, thereby reaching a substantial audience and causing severe reputational damage to the Plaintiff.

6. The Plaintiff has suffered substantial reputational harm, loss of business opportunities, and mental distress owing to the false publication.

7. The publication giving rise to the cause of action occurred on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}}, which is a single event giving rise to a single cause of action.

8. The Plaintiff has not consented to or ratified the publication, and there is no justification for the said defaming statements.

LEGAL BASIS

9. Defamation is a tort under the Law of Torts (Common Law). The Plaintiff is entitled to damages for harm caused to reputation, including compensatory and exemplary damages in appropriate cases.

10. Under the Specific Relief Act, 1963, Section 39 contemplates mandatory injunctions to prevent breach of obligations, and Section 40 provides that damages may be awarded in lieu of, or in addition to, such injunctions. The Plaintiff therefore seeks a mandatory injunction to take down the defamatory posts and damages consequent thereto.

11. The Plaintiff respectfully pleads for damages for reputational harm in addition to any incidental losses or expenses arising from the defamation.

CAUSE OF ACTION

12. The cause of action for the present suit arose on {{PUBLICATION_DATE}} when the Defendant published false and defamatory statements about the Plaintiff on social media, accusing the Plaintiff of fraud in business dealings, the statements being shared over 500 times within the jurisdiction of this Court.

13. The publication was made to third parties and was widely circulated, thereby injuring the Plaintiff's reputation in his business and social circles.

14. The Defendant acted with malice or with reckless disregard for the truth, thereby constituting defamation under the law of torts.

LIMITATION

15. The suit is within the period of limitation prescribed under the Limitation Act, 1963. The period of limitation for civil defamation is three years from the date of first publication of the defaming statements, i.e., from {{PUBLICATION_DATE}}.

RELIEF PRAYER AND DAMAGES

16. In the premises stated hereinabove, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

    (a) Award compensatory damages to the Plaintiff in the sum of Rs. 10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakh Only) for defamation and consequent reputational harm and loss of business opportunities.

    (b) Award exemplary damages in the sum of Rs. 5,00,000/- (Rupees Five Lakh Only) for willful, malicious, and wanton disregard of the Plaintiff's reputation.

    (c) Grant a mandatory injunction under Section 39 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, directing the Defendant and the social media platforms to take down and remove all defamatory posts about the Plaintiff within 24 hours of service of the order and to refrain from publishing any further defamatory content concerning the Plaintiff.

    (d) Grant such other and further relief as this Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case, including costs of the suit.
