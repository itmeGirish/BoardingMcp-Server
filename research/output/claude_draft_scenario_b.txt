JURISDICTION

1. This Hon'ble Commercial Court has jurisdiction to try and entertain the present suit inasmuch as:
   (a) The Defendant carries on business at Whitefield, Bengaluru, within the territorial jurisdiction of this Court;
   (b) The contract was executed at Bengaluru and the cause of action has arisen within the jurisdiction of this Court;
   (c) The suit claim of Rs.32,00,000/- (Rupees Thirty Two Lakh Only), being above Rs.3,00,000/-, falls within the specified value under the Commercial Courts Act, 2015;
   (d) The dispute arises out of a commercial transaction, namely a Software Development Agreement, and is therefore triable by the Commercial Court.

FACTS OF THE CASE

2. The Plaintiff is a private limited company engaged in the business of software solutions and information technology services. The Plaintiff is represented in the present proceedings by its Director, Ms. Priya Menon, duly authorized by a resolution of the Board of Directors.

3. The Defendant is a Limited Liability Partnership engaged in software development services, represented by its Designated Partner, Mr. Vikram Singh.

4. On 01.02.2024, the Plaintiff and the Defendant entered into a Software Development Agreement (hereinafter "the Agreement") for the development of an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system for the Plaintiff, for a total contract value of Rs.45,00,000/- (Rupees Forty Five Lakh Only). A true copy of the Agreement is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-A.

5. In terms of the Agreement, the Plaintiff paid Rs.20,00,000/- (Rupees Twenty Lakh Only) as the first milestone payment to the Defendant on 01.02.2024 via NEFT bearing UTR No. ICIC20240201456789. A true copy of the NEFT receipt is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-B.

6. As per the terms of the Agreement, the Defendant was obligated to deliver the first module of the ERP system on or before 01.05.2024.

7. The Defendant failed to deliver any module of the ERP system by the stipulated deadline of 01.05.2024, and despite repeated requests and follow-up communications by the Plaintiff, the Defendant completely abandoned the project on 15.06.2024 without returning the advance amount. True copies of the email correspondence evidencing the Defendant's failure and abandonment are annexed hereto and collectively marked as ANNEXURE-C.

8. As a direct and proximate consequence of the Defendant's breach and abandonment, the Plaintiff suffered an additional loss of Rs.12,00,000/- (Rupees Twelve Lakh Only) on account of delay in business operations caused by the non-delivery of the ERP system. A Loss Assessment Report prepared by the Plaintiff's auditor quantifying the said loss is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-D.

9. The total claim of the Plaintiff is Rs.32,00,000/- (Rupees Thirty Two Lakh Only), being the aggregate of:
   (a) Rs.20,00,000/- — refund of the first milestone payment; and
   (b) Rs.12,00,000/- — consequential damages on account of delay in operations.

10. The Plaintiff, through its advocate, issued a Legal Notice dated 01.07.2024 calling upon the Defendant to refund Rs.20,00,000/- and pay damages of Rs.12,00,000/- within 15 days of receipt. A true copy of the Legal Notice is annexed hereto and marked as ANNEXURE-E.

11. Despite receipt and acknowledgment of the Legal Notice, the Defendant has failed to refund the advance or pay any damages to the Plaintiff.

LEGAL BASIS

12. The Defendant's failure to deliver the ERP system as per the terms of the Agreement and the subsequent abandonment of the project constitutes a fundamental breach of contract within the meaning of Sections 37, 39, and 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

13. Under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, when a contract has been broken, the party who suffers by such breach is entitled to receive, as compensation for the loss or damage caused to him thereby, such compensation for loss or damage as naturally arose in the usual course of things from such breach, or which the parties knew, when they made the contract, to be likely to result from the breach of it.

14. The Plaintiff is entitled to:
   (a) Refund of Rs.20,00,000/- paid as advance, on account of total failure of consideration under Section 65 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872; and
   (b) Consequential damages of Rs.12,00,000/- under Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

CAUSE OF ACTION

15. The cause of action for the present suit first arose on 01.05.2024 when the Defendant failed to deliver the first module as per the Agreement, and further arose on 15.06.2024 when the Defendant abandoned the project. The cause of action is a continuing one as the Defendant continues to withhold the advance amount and refuses to compensate the Plaintiff for the losses suffered.

LIMITATION

16. The present suit is well within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 55 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963, which provides a period of three years for suits for compensation for breach of any contract, express or implied. The breach accrued on 15.06.2024 and the present suit is filed well within the prescribed period.

VALUATION AND COURT FEE

17. The suit is valued at Rs.32,00,000/- (Rupees Thirty Two Lakh Only), being the total amount claimed by the Plaintiff. The court fee is paid on the said valuation as per the provisions of the Karnataka Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1958, at the applicable ad valorem rate.

INTEREST

18. The Plaintiff claims interest at the rate of 18% per annum as follows:
   (a) Pre-suit interest on Rs.32,00,000/- from 15.06.2024 (date of breach/abandonment) till the date of filing of the present suit;
   (b) Pendente lite interest on the total amount during the pendency of this suit at such rate as this Hon'ble Court deems just and reasonable;
   (c) Future interest at the rate of 18% per annum from the date of the decree till the date of actual realization.
